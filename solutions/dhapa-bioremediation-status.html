Dhapa's 40 Lakh Tonnes: The Live Bio-Mining Reboot

White Paper No. 04 designs the system that stops new unsegregated waste from reaching a landfill in the first place. It says nothing about the 40 lakh metric tonnes already sitting at Dhapa since 1987. That's a separate, live problem — and unlike most of what this desk covers, it already has a real project underway. This annex is a status briefing, not a proposal.

1. The Backstory: A Stalled First Phase

  • The legacy mound: roughly 40 lakh (4 million) metric tonnes accumulated over three and a half decades, managed by the Kolkata Municipal Corporation (KMC) under National Green Tribunal (NGT) directives.
  • Phase I, awarded 2020: KMC contracted bio-mining of the mound under a strict deadline. COVID-19 delays and subpar execution left the contractor having processed only ~15 lakh metric tonnes — roughly 37–40% of the total.
  • Contract termination: under NGT pressure over the missed deadline, KMC terminated the agency's contract. The dispute moved to the Calcutta High Court, and fresh garbage kept piling onto the active 24-hectare zone during the pause.
  • The 2026 reboot: KMC issued fresh global tenders split into specialized phases — Phase II and Phase III, each targeting 10 lakh metric tonnes — to clear the remaining 25 lakh tonnes systematically.
The Pipeline

Microbial and mechanical separation, not heavy chemicals.

Step 1 · 3–4 weeks

Microbial Windrow Stabilization

Legacy heaps are excavated and laid out in long rows (windrows), then repeatedly sprayed with specialized bio-cultures. The microbes accelerate decomposition of any remaining organic matter, control foul odors, and reduce moisture content through natural heating.

Step 2 · Operational phase

Mechanical Aeration and Air Drying

Heavy earthmovers mechanically turn over the stabilized waste, introducing oxygen to sustain aerobic microbial activity and drying the material fully ahead of mechanical sorting.

Step 3 · Mechanical segregation

Sifting via High-Velocity Trommels

The dried matter is fed through large rotating cylindrical sieves (trommels) and ballistic separators, which use varying mesh sizes to split the bulk mass into distinct fractions by size and density.

Material Recovery

100% resource redirection, zero re-landfilling.

Refuse-Derived Fuel~5–6%Shredded textiles, dry wood & multi-layered plastics, baled and shipped to cement plants and power kilns as a coal substitute

4. The Engineered End Goal

Flattening the mounds isn't the finish line. The Jadavpur University Civil Engineering Department is consulting on the project to monitor environmental parameters and leachate pollution. Once the active 24 hectares are fully cleared, the reclaimed land converts into Kolkata's first scientifically engineered Sanitary Landfill Site — with thick geo-textile base liners and automated leachate-collection networks designed to stop toxic runoff from reaching the neighboring East Kolkata Wetlands and the water tables and farmland around them.

Bio-mining doesn't make the legacy mound disappear by magic — it disassembles 35 years of mixed waste back into fuel, fertilizer, and construction inputs, then engineers the cleared ground so it can never become the same kind of mound again. — BHP Solutions Desk
